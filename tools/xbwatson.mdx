**xbWatson** is the Xbox crash dump collection and analysis tool. When your title crashes on a dev kit, retail console in dev mode, or in a tester's hands, xbWatson is what gives you the symbolicated callstack.

## How it works

1. The console writes a crash mini-dump to its local storage whenever a title process terminates abnormally.
2. **xbWatson** pulls those dumps from one or more consoles in your studio.
3. It resolves them against your PDB symbol store and produces grouped reports by failure signature.

## When to use it

- **Pre-cert testing** — run xbWatson against the test team's consoles overnight to catch every crash from the previous play session.
- **Crash triage** — group dumps by signature so you can prioritize the most frequent crash, not the most recent one.
- **External betas** — collect dumps from playtester kits before they wipe them.

## Symbol setup

xbWatson needs PDBs to be useful. Either:

- Point it at a UNC symbol store populated by your CI build.
- Use `symsrv.dll` against the Microsoft public symbol server for Microsoft binaries.

Without PDBs you get raw RVAs; with PDBs you get function, file, and line.
