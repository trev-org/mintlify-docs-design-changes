The **Microsoft Gaming Accessibility Testing Service (MGATS)** is a free service Microsoft offers to evaluate titles against the [Xbox Accessibility Guidelines (XAG)](/principles/xbox-accessibility-guidelines).

## How it works

1. **Request MGATS** through your Microsoft contact.
2. **Provide a build** in your designated sandbox plus any docs about your accessibility features.
3. **MGATS testers evaluate** the title against XAG 101 – 123.
4. **You receive a structured report** with screenshots and remediation suggestions per finding.

## When to schedule

Run MGATS **before your final certification submission** — ideally at the same point in the project where you'd run a final QA pass. Issues identified in MGATS are easier to fix before you're locked to a release candidate.

## What MGATS does and doesn't do

**MGATS does:**

- Evaluate against XAG.
- Highlight friction with assistive technologies (screen readers, switch input, etc.).
- Suggest specific remediations, not just flag issues.

**MGATS does not:**

- Issue a pass/fail certification.
- Test legal/regulatory compliance (ADA, EAA, etc.) — the XAGs are guidance, not compliance.
- Replace your own QA's accessibility pass.

## How to act on the report

- Triage findings by XAG ID and severity.
- Fix high-impact issues before cert; document deferrals.
- Reflect the supported accessibility features in your [Properties](/publishing/properties) module so they appear on the Store PDP.
