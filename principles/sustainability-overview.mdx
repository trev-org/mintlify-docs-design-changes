Xbox is the first console platform to ship specialized tools for measuring **energy consumption** and **carbon emissions**. The Xbox Developer Sustainability Toolkit helps you understand the energy and emissions intensity of your titles and apply precision engineering to reduce both — without degrading the gameplay experience.

## Why it matters

Fossil-fuel energy used to power Xbox consoles globally is roughly **75%** of the total energy and carbon footprint of Xbox's value chain. About **50% of that** is actual gameplay. Reducing in-game energy demand benefits players directly (lower energy bills, cooler hardware, longer battery life) and the planet.

<Tip>
  **"But I'm just one game."** Assume a PC game averages 0.35 kW, played 10 h/week, with 80,000 weekly active users:

  - `0.35 kW × 10 h = 3.5 kWh / user / week`
  - `3.5 kWh × 80,000 WAU × 52 weeks = 14,560,000 kWh / year`
  - At 0.334 kg CO2e/kWh global weighted average: **~4,863 metric tonnes CO2e / year**

  That's the carbon equivalent of ~12.5M car miles or 5.7k acres of forest. A 10% improvement is meaningful.
</Tip>

## What the toolkit delivers

### GDK Power Monitor and APIs

- Real-time energy consumption telemetry down to the millisecond.
- Establish your title's baseline, measure GPU usage granularly, find inefficiencies.
- Profiling API available for games with their own pipelines.
- The Xbox Series X Dev Kit's front panel displays a **Power Load %** for quick reference.
- External power-watt meters work too.

### Dynamic Power States (DPS)

Lets the system dynamically scale the GPU's power level based on rendering workload. Enable with:

```cpp
SetDriverHintX(DRIVER_HINT_SET_DYNAMIC_POWER, 1);
```

The driver auto-selects the right p-state from recent frame-time history.

### Certification Sustainability Power Reports

The Xbox Sustainability and Certification teams can deliver targeted reports comparing your title's power use to platform averages for static menus, loading screens, and common in-game scenarios. Request via your Microsoft contact.

### Partner Center sustainability dashboards

For released titles, the Partner Center dashboards show:

- **Average power consumption** per title.
- **Global energy footprint** over time.
- **Carbon / greenhouse gas emissions** per title and per studio.

Ideal for tracking the impact of patches and content updates.

### Energy Efficiency Essentials checklist

See [**Energy Efficiency Essentials**](/principles/energy-efficiency-essentials) for the actionable checklist developed with Xbox Game Studios and the Sustainability Toolkit pilot partners.

## Two ways to add efficiency features

| Passive features | Opt-in / Opt-out features |
| --- | --- |
| Design choices that lower power use at moments the player won't notice (e.g., capping menu / lobby frame rate at 30 FPS). | Player-facing choices (e.g., an Eco Mode that renders at lower resolution). |
| No UI surface required. | Requires UI; default state can be set by the studio. |
