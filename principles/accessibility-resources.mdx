Microsoft offers a portfolio of accessibility programs, learning resources, and player-engagement frameworks for Xbox game developers. Pick the ones that match your studio's stage.

## Learning

### Gaming Accessibility Fundamentals (GAF)

A self-paced learning path on Microsoft Learn that walks developers through the why and the how of accessible game design. Recommended as the first stop for any developer new to accessibility.

### Gaming and Disability Player Experience Guide (GADPEG)

Long-form research-backed guide that explains how players with different disabilities actually experience games. Each section covers a disability category (motor, visual, cognitive, hearing, speech), describes typical assistive technology, and points to design patterns that work.

Use GADPEG to brief the design and UX leads at the start of a project, not just the engineers shipping features.

### Disability etiquette

Short plain-language guidance on language, terminology, and tone when talking about disability — internally with your team, in marketing copy, and in support channels.

## Player-facing surfaces

### Accessibility Feature Tags

A standard set of feature tags (text-to-speech, narrator support, remappable controls, color-blind modes, photosensitivity options, etc.) you declare in Partner Center. The tags surface on the Microsoft Store product page and let players filter for titles that match their needs.

Declare tags **truthfully** — the Store auditing process verifies them and tags can be revoked if they don't match the shipped experience.

### Accessibility of gaming AI experiences

Guidance specific to AI-driven game features (NPC dialog generation, adaptive difficulty, procedural narration). AI features carry their own accessibility considerations: deterministic alt-text fallbacks, opt-out paths, and ensuring TTS pipelines don't drop generated content.

## Validation and feedback

### Microsoft Gaming Accessibility Testing Service (MGATS)

Free expert evaluation of your title against the [Xbox Accessibility Guidelines](/principles/xbox-accessibility-guidelines). See the [MGATS](/principles/mgats) page for the engagement model.

### Xbox Accessibility Insiders League (XAIL)

A private flighting audience of accessibility-focused Insiders. Use [Xbox Insider Program flighting](/publishing/xbox-insider-program) to ship a pre-release build to XAIL members specifically; they validate against assistive tech you may not have on-site and file detailed structured feedback.

## Workshops and events

### Game Accessibility Workshop Developer Toolkit (GAWT)

Facilitator-ready materials for running internal accessibility workshops at your studio: slide decks, exercises, design-jam templates, and case studies. Use this when you want to onboard the whole team — not just the accessibility champion — in one session.

### Playbook for Accessible Gaming Events (PAGE)

Reference checklist and templates for running accessible community events — tournaments, launch parties, esports, content-creator showcases. Covers physical venue requirements, streaming captions, sign-language interpretation, and remote-participation accommodations.
