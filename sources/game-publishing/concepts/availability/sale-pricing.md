# Sale Pricing  

Sale Pricing is used to configure limited-time price reductions for your product. When using price reductions, the previous price has a strikethrough in the Microsoft Store.  You can choose to offer the product either at a lower price tier or with a percentage-based discount.  

> [!IMPORTANT]
> If you want to use the "Sale Pricing" section, reach out to your Microsoft contacts because this section can only be configured by Microsoft.  

## Products this applies to

* Game
* Bundle
* Add-On Bundle
* Season Pass
* Consumable (Microsoft Store-managed) or Consumable (Developer-managed)
* Durable or Durable with packages

## Does not apply to

* Game Demo  

## Use Cases  

* Price promotion or targeted promotional pricing.  

## Configuration  

* Pricing and Availability
  * Sale pricing
    * To configure "Sale Pricing," there are 4 required fields.
    * Required fields
      * Markets
      * Type
      * Start Date
      * End Date  

## Field definitions  

* Markets
  * What Markets the sale pricing should be active in.  This will only allow regions that have been selected in the pricing and availability section already (regions that the product is configured for).
* Type
  * This designation selects the main audience for the promotion.
* Start date
  * The date and time that the sale starts.
* End date
  * The date and time that the sale ends.  

## Definition of the options for the *offer to* options  

* Everyone
  * This option means that everyone who has an MSA on a Microsoft digital store will get a discount on the product.
* Owners of
  * With this selected, the second dropdown list will enumerate every Game and Bundle product for the Publisher who owns the product and that the signed-in user has access to.
  * Selecting one of these products will then only offer the sale to the owners of the selected product.
* Known User Group
  * A group or list of users who a publisher can create as a target audience.  This is a static list until it's updated.
* Segment
  * A group of dynamic users who have been identified as a targeted group across the ecosystem to receive a discount.  An example of this would be first-time purchasers.
* Subscribers
  * Users who have a current subscription, such as for Xbox Game Pass.
* Owners of Add-on
  * This allows for a Store ID to be entered for any digital gaming product in the catalog.
    * It allows for cross-publisher entitlements if needed.
  
## Use of Discounts  

* Discounts are used and leveraged in multiple ways.  You can run multiple discount scenarios on a product at the same time.  The end user will always be offered the best-case pricing.  
* Common uses are
  * Game Sales
  * Franchise Sales
  * Publisher Sales
  * Loyalty Pricing
  * Incentive Pricing
