# Configure product relationships for a game

[!INCLUDE [reminder](../includes/managed-creators-only-feature.md)]

This article describes how to configure a product relationship between game products. Configure the relationships that your product has with other products, including products that it's a generation of and products that it can use or sell, aside from its own add-ons. For more information about the types of product relationships that you can configure, see [Product relationship management](../concepts/relationship-management.md).

## Prerequisites

 - Enrollment in a Managed Creator program. If you're not enrolled yet, see [Onboarding as a new Creator](../publishing-processes/managed-creators/publishing-processes-onboarding-new-creator.md).
 - Active Partner Center account. If you don't have an account yet, speak to your Microsoft contacts.
 - Approved Game Concept for your product. If you don't have concept approval yet, see [Game Concepts](../publishing-processes/managed-creators/publishing-processes-game-concepts.md).

## Configure a product relationship

1. Sign in to [Partner Center](https://developer.microsoft.com/dashboard).
1. On the **Home** page, select **Apps and games**.
1. On the **Apps and games | Overview** page, select your title. Or, enter your game's name in the search bar and then select your game.
1. Select **Product relationship setup** from the left navigation pane.
1. To create a new relationship, select **Add new relationship**. Then, select a **Relationship type**, which can be one of the following: **can sell**, **can use and sell**, and **generation of**.
1. From the list, select all items that you want to create a product relationship for, with the base game. Enter the name or Microsoft Store ID in the search bar to filter items.
1. Select **Add**.
1. Select **Save draft**.
