# Disc packaging requirements

[!INCLUDE [reminder](../../includes/managed-creators-only-feature.md)]

To promote visual consistency across all Xbox products, all physical disc releases are required to use standard packaging templates, iconography, logos, fonts, and hardware imagery.

All assets that you might require are available for download from the Microsoft Retail Asset Distribution (RAD) portal. For more information, including download links to each asset category, see [Branding and Marketing](../../resources/managed-support/how-to-access-branding-and-marketing-assets.md).

## Creating game packaging artwork

Fully localized Xbox disc game packaging templates are available to download from RAD. 

Your game might qualify for additional front and back of pack iconography, including the Optimized for Xbox Series X and Smart Delivery feature icons. The templates include guidance and qualification criteria for display of icons on your packaging. 

Reach out to [Microsoft Art Certification Support](../../resources/managed-support/overview-microsoft-representatives-and-contacts.md#additional-contacts) with any template related questions. 

## Submitting packaging artwork for Microsoft Certification

You must submit all packaging and product related artwork to the Microsoft Art Certification team for certification before it can be released to manufacturing. This includes Finished Product Unit (FPU), Special, Limited and Collector’s Editions. 

Submissions are managed through the Apollo portal (see [Useful Links](../../resources/useful-links.md) for access). All game titles must be added to the Apollo portal before submissions can be made. For game title additions and support with Apollo, contact Microsoft Art Certification Support. If you require access to Apollo, see [Who are your Microsoft contacts?](../../resources/managed-support/overview-microsoft-representatives-and-contacts.md) for guidance on who to contact.

## Packaging requirements for Release to Manufacture (RTM)

Before an Xbox disc binary ID can be approved for RTM, you must have the following packaging materials approved in the Apollo tool.

 - Approved **Outer Wrap** component for at least one market where the physical product will be sold

If you attempt to approve a product for Release to Manufacture (RTM) prior to all necessary packaging materials being submitted and approved, the RTM request will be blocked until this is resolved. You might be required to submit additional packaging materials before you can pack-out your product at your chosen Authorized Replicator (AR).

> [!NOTE]
> When submitting physical packaging components for review and approval in the Apollo portal, ensure you list each individual country/region that the component will be distributed to as part of your physical package. Failure to disclose countries/regions accurately might result in the asset being rejected or your RTM request being blocked.
