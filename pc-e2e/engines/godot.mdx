The [Xbox Godot Sample](https://aka.ms/XBOXGodotSample) is a public, source-only reference on GitHub. It demonstrates how to build a Godot extension that integrates the Microsoft GDK, Xbox services, and PlayFab — so you can ship to Xbox on PC without leaving Godot.

## What's included

- Microsoft GDK platform services
- Xbox services (sign-in, achievements, etc.)
- PlayFab Core \+ Services
- PlayFab Multiplayer (Lobby, Matchmaking, Party)
- PlayFab Game Saves
- GameInput (with a bridge to Godot's `Input` / `InputMap`)

Compatible with the April 2026 GDK out of the box.

## What this is — and isn't

<Info>
  - **Source-only sample**, not a product. MIT-licensed at the wrapper layer; GDK and PlayFab require their own installs and license acceptance.
  - **No fixed update cadence.** Microsoft monitors the repo and iterates, but this is not a commercial release. Community PRs welcome.
  - **Xbox on PC only.** Does not include Xbox Series X|S or Xbox One support — see below.
</Info>

## Xbox Series X|S and Xbox One support

The Xbox Godot Sample is **PC-only**. Because Godot is open source and MIT-licensed, the Godot Foundation does not officially maintain console export templates — console SDKs are distributed under NDA, which is incompatible with Godot's open development model.

For current console porting details and middleware providers, see [Godot and console porting](https://godotengine.org/consoles/).

If you're shipping to Xbox Series X|S or Xbox One via Godot, contact your Xbox representative. Not yet registered? Start with [ID@Xbox onboarding](/onboarding).
