Every game on the Microsoft Store must carry an age rating for each market it ships in. Microsoft uses the **International Age Rating Coalition (IARC)** questionnaire: you fill out a single content survey in Partner Center and IARC issues ratings for every participating rating board worldwide — ESRB (North America), PEGI (Europe), USK (Germany), CERO (Japan), ACB (Australia), ClassInd (Brazil), and more.

## Where to set it

In Partner Center, open your title → **Product setup** → **Age ratings**. The IARC questionnaire walks through content categories: violence, sexual content, language, controlled substances, gambling, user interactions, and a few platform-specific extras.

Answer honestly. Misrepresenting content to obtain a lower rating is a Microsoft Store policy violation and can result in the title being delisted in affected markets.

## Special-case boards

A few markets do not accept IARC and require a separate, manual rating submission:

| Region | Board | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Germany | USK | IARC produces a provisional rating; USK may override after review. |
| South Korea | GRAC | Manual submission via Partner Center required for retail availability. |
| Mainland China | NRTA | Not covered by IARC; separate publishing path required. |

If you're not shipping to any of these markets, IARC alone is sufficient.

## Re-submission triggers

Resubmit the IARC questionnaire whenever you:

- Add a major content update that changes the content category mix (e.g., adding multiplayer where there was none, adding loot boxes, adding in-game purchases).
- Add support for a new market.
- Add user-generated content surfaces (chat, UGC sharing).

A forgotten re-rating after a content update is a common cert finding.

## In-game disclosure

If your title contains:

- **In-game purchases** — disclose in the store listing and surface a clear notice in-game on first purchase.
- **Random paid item generation (loot boxes)** — publish drop rates in-game per market requirements (Belgium, Japan, China, and others have specific rules).
- **User-to-user interaction** — declare it; this affects the rating in several markets.
