Add-ons are additional products customers can purchase for your game — in-game currency, DLC, expansions, season passes. Add-ons are published through Partner Center and must be enabled in your game's code via the [`XStore` API](/reference/xstore).

Every add-on must be associated with a game already created in Partner Center (even an unsubmitted one). Create add-ons from the **Add-ons** page in your product's navigation.

## Consumable

For things you can buy, use, and buy again (in-game currency, etc.).

- **Developer-managed**: purchasable once until your game reports it as fulfilled/consumed; then purchasable again. The game tracks the reward state.
- **Microsoft Store–managed**: each purchase grants a configured quantity that the Store tracks. When the game reports fulfillment, the Store deducts from the user's balance. Purchasable repeatedly without per-purchase fulfillment.

## Durable

One-time purchase, content included in the base game package. Used to unlock features, narrative chapters, expansion modes, vehicles, environments, character assets.

- Default product lifetime: **Forever**.
- Optional expiration: 1–365 days. After expiration, the customer can repurchase.

## Durable with packages

Like Durable but the add-on ships its own package (e.g., for physical disc inclusion or content outside the base game package). After purchase, the customer downloads and installs the package; the GDK licenses and mounts it.

## Add-on Bundle

A collection of add-ons sold as one product.

- Must include at least one product before publishing.
- Bundle contents must share the same parent product as the bundle.
- You can add Durables to a published bundle. Consumables cannot be added after publishing.

## Season Pass

A special bundle that lets customers download specified add-ons.

- Can be published **empty**.
- Can be updated to include new add-ons as they release — ideal for staged DLC.
- Create via **Add-on Bundle** → select **Make this product a Season Pass** during creation.

## Subscription

Configured by Microsoft. Contact your Microsoft representative.

## Store listings for add-ons

Store listings are optional for most add-on types. Add-ons **with** a listing appear on the Store PDP and on the parent product's PDP. Add-ons **without** a listing are buyable only from inside your game (based on the configured Schedule).
