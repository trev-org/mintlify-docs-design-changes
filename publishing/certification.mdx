Before your game can ship on Xbox or Windows PC through the Microsoft Store, it must pass Xbox Certification — a quality and compliance review process that validates your title meets all applicable Xbox Requirements (XRs). Certification exists to ensure every game in the Microsoft Store is stable, safe, and provides a consistent player experience across Xbox hardware generations. Understanding what the process involves — and where titles commonly fail — will save your team significant iteration time.

## What are Xbox Requirements?

Xbox Requirements (XRs) are the policies, technical requirements, and product component standards that all developers and publishers of Xbox console titles must meet. XRs cover areas including title stability, security, online safety, accessibility, commerce, and naming conventions. Requirements marked with an asterisk (**\***) are actively tested during certification; unmarked requirements are policy-level and may be enforced in other ways.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Console Games">
    Console XRs apply to titles shipping on Xbox One and Xbox Series X|S. Key requirement areas include:

    - **XR-001: Title Stability** — titles must start promptly, run without crashes, and shut down gracefully
    - **XR-003: Title Quality for Submission** — games must be fully functional and testable at submission
    - **XR-130: Xbox Console Families and Generations** — a title must support all devices within its target console generation
    - **XR-013: Linking Microsoft Accounts with Publisher Accounts** — titles using partner-hosted services must support linking to the player's Microsoft account
    - **XR-015: Managing Player Communication** — chat and communications must respect the player's Xbox privacy settings
    - **XR-074: Loss of Connectivity** — titles must handle Xbox and partner service outages gracefully
    - **XR-131: Display Mode Support** — Game DVR and screenshots must work correctly in all display modes

    See the full [Xbox Requirements for console games](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/gaming/gdk/docs/store/policies/console/certification-requirements?view=gdk-2604) for the complete list.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="PC Games">
    PC XRs apply to titles shipping on Windows through the Microsoft Store. Requirements cover areas like Windows compatibility, application security, store policy compliance, and Xbox services integration (if used).

    See the [Xbox Requirements for PC games](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/gaming/gdk/docs/store/policies/pc/live-policies-pc?view=gdk-2604) for the complete list.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## The certification process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare your submission">
    Before submitting, run [Submission Validator](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/gaming/gdk/docs/features/common/packaging/subval/submissionvalidator?view=gdk-2604) on your package and include the validator logs with your submission. Ensure all Partner Center sections are complete: age ratings approved, store listing filled out, and packages uploaded.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for certification in Partner Center">
    In Partner Center, navigate to your product submission and select **Submit for certification**. Your package enters the certification queue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Certification testing">
    Microsoft's certification team tests your title against all applicable XRs. They run the documented test cases, check for stability issues, validate Xbox services integration, and review store metadata.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive results">
    You receive a certification report with either a **Pass** or **Fail** result. If your submission fails, the report lists each failing test case with detailed steps to reproduce the issue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Address failures and resubmit">
    Fix the identified issues and resubmit. Resubmissions go through the same testing cycle. Use the Failure Mode Analysis process (see below) if you believe a failure is a false positive or an edge case.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Certification passed — publish">
    Once your submission passes certification, you can trigger your retail publish from Partner Center. Your game becomes available in the Microsoft Store according to your availability settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Cert Bypass program

The **Cert Bypass program** allows approved titles to publish updates that skip the full certification queue under certain conditions — enabling faster iteration for content updates, hotfixes, and non-code changes. Contact your Microsoft Developer Partner Manager to determine whether your title qualifies and to request access.

<Note>
  Cert Bypass does not waive Xbox Requirements. Content published through Cert Bypass must still comply with all XRs; the program simply reduces queue time for qualifying updates.
</Note>

## Microsoft Gaming Accessibility Testing (MGATS)

MGATS is Microsoft's accessibility testing service that evaluates games against the [Xbox Accessibility Guidelines](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/gaming/accessibility/xbox-accessibility-guidelines). While most accessibility testing is not required for certification, MGATS reports provide actionable guidance to help you reach players with disabilities and improve your game's inclusivity. Some accessibility-related XRs — such as subtitle support — are tested in certification.

## Common certification failures

The following are among the most frequently cited issues in Xbox certification failures. Review these before your first submission.

<Accordion title="XR-001: Crashes and freezes">
  Titles must not crash or hang under normal gameplay conditions. Test all code paths — especially edge cases like network loss, controller disconnect, quick resume, and suspend/resume — before submission. Crashes that occur during certification automatically fail XR-001.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="XR-003: Incomplete or untestable submission">
  Your submission must be fully functional with all features unlocked (or accompanied by a test account and setup guide if content is gated). Submissions with broken login flows, missing test credentials, or non-functional game modes will be rejected immediately.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="XR-074: Poor network error handling">
  Titles must handle Xbox network service outages gracefully. A common failure is showing "Xbox network unavailable" errors when the issue is actually with a partner service. Verify your error messages and retry logic before submitting.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="XR-131: Game DVR / screenshots broken in HDR">
  If your title supports HDR, you must render both the SDR and HDR swap chains simultaneously. Failing to do so causes the SDR swap chain (used for Game DVR, screenshots, and broadcast) to be black or corrupted.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="XR-022: Incorrect naming of Xbox features">
  All references to Xbox hardware, controllers, and network features must use the exact terminology from the official [Terminology List](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/gaming/gdk/docs/store/policies/console/console-certification-terminology?view=gdk-2604). For example, referring to the "Xbox button" incorrectly or using deprecated button names are common failures.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="XR-015: Communications not restricted by privacy settings">
  Voice and text chat must be gated by the player's Xbox privacy settings. Check these permissions at session start and whenever a new user joins, and re-check before displaying any user data or performing communication-related actions.
</Accordion>

## Failure Mode Analysis (FMA)

If your title receives a certification failure that you believe is incorrect, ambiguous, or the result of a misunderstood test case, you can initiate a **Failure Mode Analysis (FMA)** with your Microsoft Developer Partner Manager. The FMA process provides a structured forum to review the test case result, clarify the requirement, and — if the failure is confirmed valid — align on a remediation plan. FMA does not extend your certification window, but it can prevent wasted effort fixing issues that may be misclassified.

## Beta and Game Preview requirements

Games entering the Microsoft Store as Beta or in Game Preview have specific additional requirements:

- The Beta or Game Preview state must be clearly disclosed in the store listing.
- Feedback mechanisms must be available to players.
- Crash rates and stability standards still apply during Beta/Game Preview periods.

Consult the [Beta and Game Preview requirements](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/gaming/gdk/docs/store/policies/certification-requirements-beta-game-preview?view=gdk-2604) document for the full set of rules that apply to pre-release programs.
