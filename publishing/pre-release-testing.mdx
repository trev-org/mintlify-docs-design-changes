There are four supported ways to put your game in players' hands before the retail launch. Pick the one (or ones) that match your studio size, the maturity of the build, and your marketing strategy.

## 1. Private testing with sandbox codes

The simplest path: distribute pre-release builds inside your own Xbox **sandbox**. Internal team and approved partners switch their console or PC into the sandbox via the **Dev Account Tool** and download the build from the sandboxed Microsoft Store.

Use this for daily dev builds, QA, and partner reviews. The audience is small (gated by Xbox account allowlist) and there is no store listing.

## 2. Microsoft Store flighting

Flighting lets you ship a beta build to a managed group of retail players through a separate **flight package** in Partner Center. Players opt in via a flight code, install the flight build alongside the retail build, and remain on it until you publish a new flight or move them to retail.

Ideal for:

- Public beta tests with thousands of players
- A/B testing significant gameplay changes
- Pre-release content seeding

Flight packages go through certification, but a lighter-weight version of it.

## 3. Xbox Game Preview

Game Preview is the official paid early-access program on Xbox and the Microsoft Store. Players pay the listed price and get the in-development title; you commit to ongoing updates and a clear roadmap toward 1.0.

To participate you must:

- Apply to the Game Preview program through Partner Center.
- Meet a separate set of certification requirements designed for early-access builds.
- Add a **Game Preview** badge to your store listing.
- Disclose roadmap and content gaps in the listing description.

Game Preview is appropriate for survival, simulation, and live-service titles that benefit from a long public beta with paid players.

## 4. Closed and open betas via flights or Game Preview

Use store flighting for closed betas. Use Game Preview for paid extended early access. Open betas — free, anyone can join — are typically delivered via a separate free product with a clearly-labeled "Beta" suffix in the title, on its own release schedule.

## Pre-order vs. early access

Pre-order (see [Pricing & Availability](/publishing/pricing-availability)) charges the player on the release date. Early access ships the player a build today and they keep playing through retail. These are different programs; do not confuse them.
