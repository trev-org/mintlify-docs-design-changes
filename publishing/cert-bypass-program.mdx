Cert Bypass is a Microsoft program that lets trusted publishers ship qualifying updates **without** going through the full Xbox certification queue. For live-ops titles that ship frequent patches, hotfixes, or rapid balance changes, Cert Bypass turns a multi-day cert turnaround into a same-day publish.

<Note>
  Eligibility, terms, and the application process are managed by your Microsoft representative. This page summarizes the program for planning; the official details and approval flow live in [Partner Center](/publishing/partner-center).
</Note>

## What Cert Bypass covers

When your title is enrolled in Cert Bypass for a given update channel, updates within scope skip the full XR test pass and publish through an expedited path. Microsoft retains the right to pull the title or revoke Cert Bypass eligibility if a published update is found to violate XRs after the fact.

Typical in-scope updates:

- Server-side balance changes that don't touch the executable
- Hotfix patches that fix specific bugs without adding features
- Small content drops within an existing live-ops cadence
- Localization fixes

Out of scope (still requires full cert):

- New executables on a new console SKU
- New first-time-user experiences
- New monetization mechanics
- Updates that change age-rating-relevant content
- Changes to multiplayer matchmaking or networking that affect privacy or safety

## Eligibility

Cert Bypass is offered to titles with:

- A clean cert history (low XR failure rate)
- A live-ops profile that benefits from rapid iteration
- A studio commitment to internal QA and XR self-review processes

New titles aren't eligible at launch. Establish a track record of clean submissions first.

## How to apply

Work with your Microsoft contact (Account Manager or Developer Account Manager). They submit a request on your behalf, Microsoft reviews your cert history and operational readiness, and you receive enrollment confirmation in Partner Center. Cert Bypass is granted **per title**, not per studio — each new title applies separately.

## Publishing through Cert Bypass

The publish flow in Partner Center is unchanged. After upload, the package routes to the Cert Bypass queue instead of the standard queue. You'll see the expedited status reflected in the submission timeline. Microsoft can still escalate any submission back to full cert at its discretion.

## What to do if you lose eligibility

If a Cert Bypass update is found to violate XRs after publish, Microsoft can:

1. Pull the offending update.
2. Suspend Cert Bypass for the title pending a remediation plan.
3. In serious cases, revoke Cert Bypass enrollment.

Maintain a strong internal XR self-test process before every submission to protect your eligibility.
