Every Xbox title gets a **Game Hub** — a public landing page on Xbox and the Xbox app that aggregates news, achievements, screenshots, clips, leaderboards, and community discussion for the title. Multi-title franchises can additionally configure a **Franchise Hub** that rolls up across the series.

Hubs are managed in Partner Center under **Xbox services → Game Hub** for the product.

## What's in a Game Hub

  <Card title="News" icon="newspaper">
    Studio posts that appear on the hub and in players' activity feeds. Use for patch notes, season launches, and community events.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Player content" icon="video">
    Screenshots, clips, and broadcasts captured by players. Automatically populated; you don't author it.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Achievements" icon="trophy">
    Pulled from your Xbox services configuration. Players see global completion stats and their own progress.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Leaderboards" icon="ranking-star">
    Featured stats configured in Partner Center. Surfaced on the hub and on profile cards.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The rest of the hub — friends-playing-this-title, recent achievements, top clips — is populated automatically from Xbox services data.

## Configuring the hub

1. **Banner and key art.** Upload a hero image (1920×1080 minimum) and a square hub tile (1080×1080). These can differ from your store-listing assets.
2. **About section.** A short studio-authored description of the game shown at the top of the hub.
3. **Featured stats.** Select up to three stats from your Xbox services config to feature as leaderboards.
4. **News feed.** Drafted in Partner Center, scheduled, published. Each post can be targeted to specific markets.

## Franchise Hubs

If you publish multiple Xbox titles in the same franchise, configure a Franchise Hub to roll all of them up. Players visiting the franchise hub see news and content from every member title in one place. Franchise Hub configuration is a separate flow in Partner Center; ask your Microsoft contact to enable it for your franchise.

## Activity feeds

News posts on your hub also publish into the activity feeds of players who follow the title. Investing in a regular news cadence (weekly during live-ops, patch notes per release) measurably improves retention and re-engagement.
