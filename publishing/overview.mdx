Publishing a game to the Microsoft Store is an end-to-end process that takes you from initial developer enrollment through certification and into retail availability. Whether you're shipping on Xbox Series X|S, Xbox One, Windows PC, or all three, the same core workflow applies: join a creator program, onboard in Partner Center, configure your game, pass certification, and publish. This page walks you through each stage and points you to the detailed guides for each step.

## Creator tracks

Microsoft offers two paths for developers who want to publish games on Xbox and Windows:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="ID@Xbox (Managed Creators)">
    ID@Xbox is the managed creator program for independent game developers. It provides access to the full suite of Xbox development tools, including the GDK, advanced store features, Xbox services integrations, and dedicated account management support from Microsoft.

    **Best for:** titles seeking the broadest platform access, Xbox services features (achievements, multiplayer, cloud saves), and marketing opportunities.

    [Apply for ID@Xbox](https://www.xbox.com/Developers/id)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Xbox Creators Program">
    The Xbox Creators Program offers a simplified publishing path with streamlined certification. It does not require Microsoft approval to publish, but has more limited access to Xbox services and store features.

    **Best for:** smaller titles or rapid prototyping where full Xbox services integration is not required.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## End-to-end publishing flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Join a developer program">
    Apply for ID@Xbox or enroll in the Xbox Creators Program. Once approved, you receive access to Partner Center and the GDK download site.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register and onboard in Partner Center">
    [Partner Center](https://partner.microsoft.com/dashboard) is the central hub for all publishing operations. Create your developer account, configure your organization details, and invite any team members who need access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your game product">
    In Partner Center, create a new game product. This generates your Store ID and Xbox Title ID, which you will configure in `MicrosoftGame.config` in your GDK project.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Game Setup">
    Complete the Game Setup section: set your supported platforms (Xbox, Windows PC), configure Smart Delivery if needed, and specify device families.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set age ratings">
    Submit your game to the required rating bodies (ESRB, PEGI, and others as needed for your release markets). Partner Center guides you through the questionnaire for each rating organization.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build and upload packages">
    Build your game packages using the GDK makepkg toolchain and upload them to Partner Center. Associate the correct package with the product submission.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Xbox services">
    If your game uses Xbox services (achievements, leaderboards, multiplayer, cloud saves), configure them in the Xbox services section of Partner Center and publish the service configuration to your sandbox.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your store listing">
    Write your store description, upload screenshots, trailers, and artwork, and configure pricing and availability — including which markets you want to reach and your release date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for certification">
    Submit your package for Xbox Certification. The certification process validates that your title meets all Xbox Requirements (XRs). Review common failure modes before submitting to reduce rework.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish to retail">
    Once certification passes, trigger your retail publish. The game becomes available in the Microsoft Store according to your configured availability settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key milestones at a glance

| Milestone | Description |
|---|---|
| Product creation | Store ID and Title ID generated in Partner Center |
| Sandbox configuration | Xbox services config published to your sandbox |
| Age ratings completed | All required rating bodies approved |
| Package uploaded | Validated GDK package associated with submission |
| Certification passed | All Xbox Requirements verified |
| Retail publish | Game live in the Microsoft Store |
