This guide covers game publishing for Xbox and how to use [Microsoft Partner Center](https://partner.microsoft.com/dashboard) to create, manage, and publish your Xbox products to the Microsoft Store.

<Note>
  If there's any conflict between this documentation and an agreement you signed with Microsoft, **the agreement governs**.
</Note>

## Who this guide is for

Creators registered with Microsoft for Xbox game development and publishing, including [ID@Xbox](/onboarding) members.

If you're publishing an app (not an Xbox game) or aren't a Managed Creator, follow [Get started with the Microsoft Store](https://learn.microsoft.com/windows/apps/publish) instead.

## What Partner Center does

Partner Center is the web portal for all Microsoft Store product configuration and publishing — games, bundles, add-ons.

In Partner Center you'll:

- Create your game product and reserve a name.
- Configure Xbox services (achievements, multiplayer, leaderboards).
- Upload packages for each supported platform.
- Complete age ratings, Store listing metadata, and pricing.
- Submit for **Certification**.
- Publish to the Microsoft Store.

After launch, Partner Center also lets you:

- Respond to ratings and reviews.
- Flight packages to specific audiences.
- Access reports on product health and customer engagement.

## Team roles

| Team | Typical Partner Center work |
| --- | --- |
| **Management & publishing** | Pricing, release markets, release dates, product metadata, Store listing info, age ratings, sales monitoring |
| **Creative** | Art assets, Store listing imagery, promotional materials |
| **Development** | Xbox services config, publish packages to sandboxes, create add-ons, upload packages for cert and release |
