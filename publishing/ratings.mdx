Every game shipped on the Microsoft Store must carry the right age rating for each market it lists in. Partner Center collects this information through the **International Age Rating Coalition (IARC)** questionnaire — a single survey that produces ratings from ESRB, PEGI, USK, Russian RARS, Brazilian ClassInd, ACB (Australia), and other regional boards in one pass.

## How IARC works

You fill in one questionnaire describing the content of your game (violence, language, sexual content, gambling-like mechanics, in-game purchases, user-generated content, location data, etc.). IARC partner boards consume that answer set and each issues a rating valid for their region. Boards that don't accept IARC ratings (CERO in Japan and, for some categories, GRAC in Korea) require a separate submission outside Partner Center.

## When to submit

Complete the age-ratings questionnaire **before** you submit your game for Xbox certification. Cert blocks on missing ratings, and adding markets after the fact is much harder than getting it right the first time.

## Filling in the questionnaire

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Age Ratings in Partner Center">
    From your game product, navigate to **Submission → Age ratings** and start a new questionnaire.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Answer truthfully">
    Honest answers protect you legally and commercially. Misrepresenting content is grounds for a board to revoke the rating after launch, which pulls your title from store listings in that region.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review per-board outputs">
    Before submitting, review the rating each board will issue. If a rating is more restrictive than you expected, your product will be unavailable to younger players in that market.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    IARC issues ratings within a few minutes for most boards. Some, like USK, can take longer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## CERO and GRAC

If you target Japan or certain product types in Korea, request your CERO and GRAC ratings directly with those boards in parallel with your IARC submission. Upload the resulting certificates to Partner Center.

## In-game features that raise rating tiers

A few features almost always push your rating up:

- **Real-money in-game purchases** — PEGI adds the IGP descriptor; some boards raise the age tier.
- **Loot boxes and randomized rewards** — increasing regulatory focus; many boards now require explicit disclosure.
- **User-generated content and chat** — boards add an "Unrestricted online interactions" notice.
- **Gambling mechanics** — even simulated, this can push your rating to the maximum tier or block markets entirely.

If any of these apply, plan for the rating impact early in production.

## Updating after launch

If you patch in new content that changes the rating-relevant facts (e.g. adding user-to-user chat, loot boxes, or strong language), update the IARC questionnaire and resubmit. Failing to do so risks board takedowns.
