Your store listing is the storefront for your game on every Xbox console, Windows PC, and the web. It's the page players see when they search, get a friend's recommendation, or follow a promo link. Partner Center walks you through one listing per language, plus product-wide gaming metadata and marketing assets.

## Anatomy of a listing

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Title and description" icon="text">
    Localized name, short summary, full description, and feature list bullets. Required per language.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Screenshots and trailers" icon="image">
    1080p\+ screenshots, optional 4K hero images, and one or more trailers. Each market can have its own asset set if needed.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Store logos" icon="square">
    Square 1080×1080, wide 1920×1080, and additional tile sizes. Used across the Xbox dash and the web store.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Gaming metadata" icon="tags">
    Genre, themes, modes, players supported, accessibility features, devices supported. Powers search and discovery filters.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Required assets at a glance

| Asset | Aspect ratio | Min resolution | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hero / key art | 16:9 | 1920×1080 | The big image at the top of the listing |
| Screenshots | 16:9 | 1920×1080 | 4 minimum, 8 recommended |
| Square boxshot | 1:1 | 1080×1080 | Game tile across Xbox dashboards |
| Trailer | 16:9 | 1080p | Captured in-engine; cinematics allowed only if clearly labeled |

Deliver assets in PNG (lossless) for stills and MP4 (H.264, AAC) for trailers.

## Per-language vs. shared assets

Text is per language. Images can be shared across all markets **unless** they contain text in the image itself (e.g. a screenshot of the UI). If your screenshots have UI text, capture localized versions for every supported language; otherwise expect a cert failure on the corresponding listings.

## Description style guide

- Lead with the gameplay hook in the first sentence — it's the only sentence shown on Xbox tiles.
- Avoid superlatives that are hard to verify ("the greatest game ever").
- Don't reference competitor titles or platforms by name.
- Use the proper trademark forms for Microsoft Store, Xbox Series X|S, Xbox One, and PC Game Pass.
- Keep feature list bullets short (≤ 80 characters); they're rendered as a list on the listing.

## Gaming metadata

Fill in every applicable field — it directly affects discovery:

- **Genre and category** — used for category browse pages.
- **Player modes** — single-player, online multiplayer, cooperative campaign, etc.
- **Accessibility features** — surfaced on the listing's accessibility section.
- **Devices supported** — console, PC, cloud, handheld. Drives Smart Delivery and Xbox Play Anywhere indicators.
- **Cross-play / cross-save** — surfaced as badges where supported.

## Cert and review

Store listings go through a separate review step from the package build. They can be updated independently of game code once the title is live; minor copy fixes typically clear within a few hours.
