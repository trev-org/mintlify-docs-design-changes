**Xbox Requirements (XRs)** are the certification policies every Xbox title must meet. They cover stability, content quality, services usage, privacy, multiplayer behavior, and platform-specific rules for Xbox Series X|S generations.

Use this page as the canonical index. Each XR is tested during [certification](/publishing/certification); the [Failure Mode Analysis](/publishing/xr-fma) covers what to fix when a test fails.

## Why XRs exist

XRs ensure every Xbox title:

- Doesn't crash on basic activation, sign-in, or controller-add flows.
- Respects user privacy, communication preferences, and content settings.
- Honors multiplayer guarantees other titles depend on (joinable sessions, presence, invites).
- Behaves correctly across console generations and during DLC install / install / Quick Resume.
- Meets store-listing quality for art, metadata, and pricing display.

## How to read an XR

Each XR is:

- **Numbered** (XR-001, XR-007, etc.) and **named** for its concern.
- **Tested or untested** — some XRs are documentation requirements (publisher policy); most have explicit cert tests.
- **Per-platform** — most apply to both Xbox console and PC; some are platform-specific.
- **Versioned** — XRs change over time. Track the change history before resubmission.

## Beta and Game Preview

Xbox Game Preview and Beta titles have a tailored subset of XRs that allow incomplete features in clearly-labeled programs. See the Beta and Game Preview supporting docs for which XRs are waived and which are still tested.

## PC-specific XRs

Windows PC builds add additional XRs for:

- Cross-network data usage (XR-007 PC variant).
- Xbox Network terminology for PC and mobile.
- Top failing test cases for PC.
- Xbox Network Services data-processing addendum (privacy compliance for PC and mobile).

## Change history

XRs are revised over time. Before resubmitting, check the latest console XR change history in Partner Center to confirm whether a new test was added in your build's timeframe.
