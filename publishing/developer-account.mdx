Your **Partner Center account** is the root of everything you do as a publisher. Configure it once at the start and revisit only when team membership, billing, or tax info changes.

## Account types

- **Individual** — for solo developers. Limited to a subset of program features.
- **Company** — for studios and publishers. Required for Xbox console publishing, Game Pass agreements, and most enterprise programs.

Xbox Managed Creator titles require a Company account associated with the legal entity that signed the Microsoft publishing agreement.

## Roles

Partner Center uses **role-based access control**:

- **Owner** — full administrative access including billing and legal.
- **Manager** — manage products and submissions but not billing.
- **Developer** — upload packages, edit metadata, run analytics, but not submit to certification.
- **Custom roles** — fine-grained permissions for specific modules.

Assign the minimum role each teammate needs. Owner access should be limited to the smallest possible group at your studio.

## Tax and payout

The Microsoft Store cannot pay royalties until tax and payout profiles are complete. Configure both before you submit your first product — not at launch.

- **Tax profile** — country / region, business legal name, and Form W-8 or W-9 equivalents.
- **Payout profile** — bank account that receives royalty payments. Currency and threshold are configurable per program.

## Relationship management

If your studio is part of a publishing family or has signed multiple agreements with Microsoft, **Relationship management** is where you accept or change the publisher relationship that scopes a title. This is also where you initiate **game reparenting** — moving a published title from one Partner Center account to another (typical after an acquisition or licensing change).
