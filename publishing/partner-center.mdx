Partner Center is the web-based dashboard where all publishing operations for your Xbox and Windows PC games take place. From here you create game products, configure Xbox services, submit packages for certification, manage pricing, and monitor your title's performance after launch. Every developer who ships a game through the Microsoft Store — whether through ID@Xbox or the Xbox Creators Program — works through Partner Center throughout the entire lifecycle of their title.

## Registering as a developer

Before you can create a game product, you need a Partner Center developer account associated with your creator program membership.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Apply for your creator program">
    Apply for [ID@Xbox](https://www.xbox.com/Developers/id) (managed creators) or enroll in the Xbox Creators Program. You will receive an invitation to access Partner Center once your application is approved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your Partner Center account">
    Visit [partner.microsoft.com](https://partner.microsoft.com/dashboard) and sign in with the Microsoft account linked to your developer program invitation. Complete the account registration form, including your legal business name, contact information, and payout details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review account types and fees">
    Partner Center supports both individual and company accounts. Account registration fees vary by account type and country/region. Review the [account types, locations, and fees](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/gaming/game-publishing/concepts/developer-account) page for current pricing before completing registration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite team members">
    Navigate to **Account settings → Users** to invite additional developers, artists, producers, or QA team members. Assign each person the appropriate role — Manager, Developer, Marketer, or Analyst — to control which Partner Center sections they can access.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Setting up a new game product

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the product">
    From the Partner Center overview page, select **Create a new game**. Enter your game's name and select the target platforms (Xbox, PC, or both). Partner Center generates a **Store ID** (a 12-character alphanumeric string) and an **Xbox Title ID** for your product.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Game Setup">
    Open **Game Setup** and specify your supported console generations, enable Smart Delivery if you are shipping on both Xbox One and Xbox Series X|S, and configure any platform-specific settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Xbox services">
    In the **Xbox services** section, enable the services your game uses (for example, achievements, multiplayer, or cloud saves). Define your achievement list, leaderboard configurations, and session templates, then publish the service configuration to your **development sandbox**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set age ratings">
    Navigate to **Age ratings** and complete the questionnaire for each required rating organization. Submit the questionnaires and monitor Partner Center for approval status. You cannot submit for certification until all required ratings are approved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload your game package">
    Build your GDK package and run Submission Validator. Upload the validated `.xvc` or `.msixvc` package file to the **Packages** section and associate it with your current submission.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your store listing">
    Go to **Store listings** and provide localized text and assets for each market you are targeting. Fill in the short description, long description, keywords, and upload all required artwork sizes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure pricing and availability">
    Set your base price, choose which markets to release in, configure visibility (immediate or manual), and set your release date. You can also configure pre-order settings or a Game Preview period here.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for certification">
    When all sections have a green checkmark, submit your product for Xbox Certification. See the [Certification guide](/publishing/certification) for what to expect.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Insights and analytics

After your game ships, Partner Center's **Insights** section provides detailed reports covering:

- **Acquisitions** — units sold and downloaded over time, broken down by market and platform
- **Usage** — daily and monthly active users, session counts, and play duration
- **Health** — crash rates, errors, and performance metrics from live devices
- **Revenue** — gross and net revenue, refunds, and add-on sales

<Tip>
  You can grant team members the **Analyst** role to give them read-only access to Insights without allowing them to modify product configuration or submit packages.
</Tip>
