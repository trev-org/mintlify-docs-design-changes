Your Microsoft Store listing is the marketing surface that converts an interested player into a purchaser. It is also one of the highest-leverage things you can iterate on after launch: small improvements to the title's hero copy, key art, or feature list show up directly in conversion data.

## What a listing contains

Every listing language requires:

| Field | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| **Title name** | Localized per market. Length limits enforced. |
| **Short description** | One-line hook shown in search results. |
| **Long description** | Full marketing copy. Markdown formatting supported. |
| **Features list** | Bulleted feature highlights. |
| **Screenshots** | 1–10 per platform. Minimum 1920×1080. |
| **Trailers** | Optional but strongly recommended. Hosted by Microsoft. |
| **Hero / store art** | Multiple sizes for different placements. |
| **Search terms** | Hidden keywords for store search. |
| **Copyright notice** | Required. |

Upload distinct screenshot sets per device family (Xbox console, PC, mobile) so the store renders the right images depending on where the player is browsing.

## Per-language requirements

For every language declared in your `MicrosoftGame.config` `<Resources>` list, you must provide a localized listing. Machine-translated copy is a frequent reason for cert pushback — invest in human translation for at least the title name, short description, and feature bullets.

## Hero art best practices

- **Clean focal point.** The hero card crops to several aspect ratios. Keep the central 50% safe.
- **Avoid embedded text** other than the logo. The store overlays its own UI on top.
- **Differentiate from competitors.** Browse the store category your title will sit in and design something that stands out.

## Trailer guidelines

Upload trailers as MP4 (H.264, 1080p or 4K, 30 or 60 FPS). The first 5 seconds matter most — the store autoplays muted, and most viewers bail in the first 10 seconds.

## Iterating after launch

Store listings can be updated independently of the title's package. Treat the listing as a living asset: A/B test new screenshots, swap key art for seasonal events, refresh the feature list when you add big content updates.
