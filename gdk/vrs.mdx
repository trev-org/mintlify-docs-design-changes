**Variable Rate Shading (VRS)** lets you reduce shading rate (the number of shader invocations per pixel) in regions where the visual difference is imperceptible. The Xbox Series X|S hardware supports the same VRS surface as PC D3D12 with native rate combiners and image-based VRS.

## Why VRS

Shading cost dominates most modern frames. VRS lets you keep full-rate shading where the eye notices — character faces, near foliage, sharp specular reflections — and drop to 1×2, 2×2, or 4×4 rates in regions that don't (motion-blurred areas, dark scenes, periphery for foveated rendering).

Typical wins: 10–20% frame-time reduction with no visible quality loss when applied carefully.

## VRS tiers

| Tier | What you get |
| --- | --- |
| Tier 1 | Per-draw VRS — set rate on `IASetPipelineState`. Cheap, coarse. |
| Tier 2 | Per-primitive VRS via SV\_ShadingRate \+ image-based VRS via a screen-space texture. Fine-grained. |

Xbox Series X|S supports Tier 2.

## API surface

- `RSSetShadingRate(rate, combiners)` — per-draw shading rate.
- `RSSetShadingRateImage(image)` — binds a screen-tile texture (typically 8×8 or 16×16 tiles) that supplies a rate per region.
- `SV_ShadingRate` semantic — written from the vertex / mesh shader for per-primitive control.
- **Combiners** — per-stage merging of the per-draw, per-primitive, and per-image rates (passthrough, override, min, max, sum).

## Where to apply VRS

Practical approaches:

- **Motion-based** — build a velocity buffer; reduce rate where pixel velocity is high.
- **Luminance-based** — reduce rate in dark or saturated areas.
- **Distance / depth-based** — reduce rate at long range or behind DoF.
- **Foveated (cloud streaming)** — reduce rate at frame periphery for cloud-streamed players, knowing the encoder will compress those regions hard anyway.
- **Material-based** — selectively reduce rate per-material via `SV_ShadingRate`.

## Best practices

1. **Author the VRS image at quarter resolution or coarser** — saves bandwidth.
2. **Update VRS image early in the frame** — don't read from it the same frame you write it.
3. **Combine with TAA carefully** — TAA can hide VRS artifacts but can also magnify temporal flicker on hard edges.
4. **Profile against shading-rate-1×1 baseline in PIX** — confirm wins are real, not measurement noise.
5. **A/B with screenshots** — set aside artist time to compare rate masks.
